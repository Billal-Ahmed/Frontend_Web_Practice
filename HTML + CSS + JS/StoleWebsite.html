Mantokha Waterfall Photography

Mantokha Waterfall is one of the largest and longest waterfalls in the Skardu Valley. A few minutes’ walk will take you to this beautiful waterfall along its stream. During the monsoon season, the Skardu Mantokha Waterfalls are at their most stunning and captivating.

This waterfall is situated southeast of Skardu on the way to Shigar Valley. It is almost an hour from the main city of Skardu, with a perfect route. The way to Manthoka Waterfall is filled with lush green forests and beautiful mountainous landscapes. The waterfall is approximately 180 feet high and therefore glorious.

Explore Kachura Lake

A major offbeat tourist destination in Gilgit Baltistan, it is the base camp for K-2 expeditions. Notably, the Alpine touch, hazy weather, and perpetual snowfall make this valley look even more beautiful in winter.

Many beautiful resorts are situated on the bank of this lake with different price ranges and scenic views from each room. This place is perfect for new couples, families, and kids to make memories and have a good time. This lake is one of the must-visit places in Skardu, along with the Soq Valley.

Deosai Plain – Brown Bear Sightseeing

A natural wonderland with abundant adventure, Deosai Plain is an ideal destination for nature lovers and thrill-seekers. Enjoying the tranquility, you can explore the valley on foot or take a pony ride in this panoramic landscape.

A 3 to 4-hour thrilling off-road journey will take you to the second-highest plateau (14,000 feet above sea level). It is advisable to hire a Prado for your visit to the Deosai Plains, as the route is rugged and uneven.

For a day trip from Skardu, a 5-door Prado rental costs between Rs.15,000 and Rs.20,000 and accommodates 5 to 6 passengers. This option allows for a comfortable and enjoyable exploration of the stunning Deosai Plain.

Visit Ancient Khaplu Palace

Poplar forests and snow-covered mountains surround the Khaplu Valley, a perfect tourist destination. Khaplu Palace is one of the oldest forts in Khaplu Valley, a 4 – 5 hour drive from Skardu main City. The words peace and beauty reside in Khaplu Valley.

Located 113.4 km from Skardu, Khaplu has been attracting tourists from around the world to enjoy moments of calm. The valley boasts its rich culture and traditions. How can I get to Skardu Valley?

To get to Skardu directly from Islamabad, you must travel 700 km by road via Babusar Pass; from Lahore, 1220 km, which takes 12 to 18 hours, respectively. Once you are in Islamabad, the capital of Pakistan, you have two options for traveling to Skardu.

By taking a direct flight

The first is to fly directly to Skardu airport, a scenic 1-hour flight over the Karakoram and Himalayan Ranges. The air distance from Islamabad to Skardu is about 318 km (198 miles). You can hire local cars and Prados to explore Skardu’s tourist attractions on a per-day rental basis.

By Public Bus

You can ride public transport, such as Faisal Movers, K-2, and Mashaburam Travel buses, at affordable prices along the Karakoram Highway. The ticket price is between 4,500 and 5,000 per person, one way. On the way, you can explore the beauty of the Karakoram ranges, Nanga Parbat View, Kohistan, and the Indus River. There´s no direct bus from Lahore to Skardu, so you must go to Islamabad first.

By Private Car

If you love to travel separately by car, you can buy a seat or hire a full car with an expert driver from Islamabad to reach Skardu. It is advisable not to go directly to Skardu from Islamabad. You should take some nights in Naran or in Chillas while going to Skardu. In a car, per seat, it costs you 7000 to 9000 on one side.
